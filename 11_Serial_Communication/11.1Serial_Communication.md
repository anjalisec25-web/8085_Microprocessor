# Serial Communication in 8085

## What is Serial Communication?

Serial communication is the process of transmitting or receiving **one bit of data at a time** between the microprocessor and external devices.

---

## Serial Communication Pins

### SID (Serial Input Data)

- Used to receive serial data.
- Data enters the microprocessor through this pin.

---

### SOD (Serial Output Data)

- Used to transmit serial data.
- Data is sent from the microprocessor through this pin.

---

## Serial Communication Instructions

The 8085 uses the following instructions for serial communication:

- **SIM (Set Interrupt Mask)** – Used to control the SOD pin.
- **RIM (Read Interrupt Mask)** – Used to read data from the SID pin.

---

## Advantages

- Requires fewer wires.
- Suitable for long-distance communication.
- Simple and cost-effective.
