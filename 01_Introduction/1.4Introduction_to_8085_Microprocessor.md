# Introduction to 8085

## Definition

The **8085 Microprocessor** is an **8-bit microprocessor** developed by **Intel** in **1976**. It is a programmable electronic device that executes instructions, processes data, and controls the operation of a computer system.

## Why is it called 8085?

- **80** represents the processor series.
- **85** indicates that it operates on a **5V power supply**.

## Characteristics

- 8-bit microprocessor.
- 16-bit address bus.
- 8-bit data bus.
- Can access up to **64 KB** of memory.
- Available in a **40-pin** DIP package.
- Operates on a **+5V** power supply.

## Summary

The 8085 is a simple and widely used microprocessor that is mainly used for learning the fundamentals of microprocessor architecture and assembly language programming.
