# What is a Microcontroller?

## Definition

A **microcontroller** is a compact integrated circuit (IC) that contains a **CPU, memory, and input/output (I/O) ports** on a single chip. It is designed to perform specific tasks in embedded systems.

## Characteristics

- CPU, memory, and I/O ports are integrated on a single chip.
- Designed for dedicated applications.
- Low power consumption.
- Compact and cost-effective.

## Advantages

- Low power consumption
- Compact size
- Low cost
- Easy to use in embedded systems

## Disadvantages

- Limited memory
- Lower processing power than a microprocessor
- Suitable for specific tasks only
